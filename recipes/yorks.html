Yorkshire pudding

This is a recipe for a classic British side-dish: Yorkshire pudding. It is usually served with gravy and Sunday roast, but can be eaten with variety of dishes.

This recipe makes 8 large puds.

Ingredients

  • Plain flour: 140g
  • Eggs: 4
  • Milk: 200ml
  • Sunflower oil
  • Salt and pepper: to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 210 degrees Celsius
  2. Drizzle the Yorkshire pudding tin with sunflower oil and put into the oven to heat through
  3. Beat the eggs into the flour in a container, until smooth
  4. Gradually add milk, continuing to beat, until there are no lumps left
  5. Season with salt and pepper
  6. Carefully and evenly pour the batter into the tin holes
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until puffed-up and browned
